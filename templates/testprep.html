Although the content of each is relatively similar, there are a few major differences that you should consider in order to see which test appeals to you. One factor to consider is the differences between the types of sections in the tests. For example, the ACT has a science section while the SAT doesn't. The SAT also has a math section where you are not allowed to use a calculator while on the ACT you can use the calculator for every math question. The timing for the sections also varies and can make a big difference.

Once you know when you are taking the test, it's time to start cracking! Everyone is different so it is important to figure out which study methods work for you. SAT Subject Tests

Why yshould I take SAT Subject Tests?

1. Colleges may require subject tests
List of colleges that use SAT 2’s : https://collegereadiness.collegeboard.org/sat-subject-tests/about/institutions-using

2. It’ll help you stand out in the college process
Even in your dream college doesn’t require any subject tests, doing well on one or more tests can help you stand out from your competition.

3. Show colleges your interests
You can take subject tests related to your majors to show how you are ready to continue studying a certain subject in college.

4. Fulfill requirements or get credit
Some colleges use subject tests to place students in certain courses. Doing well on subject tests may allow you to get credit for introductory-level courses.
