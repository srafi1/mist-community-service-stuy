COLLEGE PREP

OVERVIEW & PURPOSE

        College process? Confused? Nervous? Not sure what you need to do? Well read on!
The college process starts essentially freshmen year. The classes you take, extracurriculars you choose to do and your standardized tests will all be factored in. Look at the other pages on this website to learn more about those aspects in detail. This page will focus on how to apply to college and tips and tricks to help you get into your dream college.




Getting Started

Build up academic success in order to be prepared for college!

  • Improve time management skills
  • Don't settle for easy classes; challenge yourself whether it be by taking CollegeNow classes or Advanced Placement classes
  • Talk to your guidance counselor to make sure you are on track to graduating and are fulfilling the graduation requirements for your high-school
  • Explore extracurriculars such as clubs, jobs, community service, sport and internships. Choose a few and stick to them. Continuity and improvement such as leadership or achievements will show your skills to college. Make your summers count!
  • Maintain good relationships with teachers and guidance counselor. They will be the ones writing your letters of recommendation to colleges. Most colleges require 2 letters of recommendation from teacher plus counselor recommendation. It is recommended to get one from a STEM teacher (junior/senior year) and the other from a humanities teacher(junior/senior year)
  • Start thinking about what you wish to study in

College Checklist

*Keep deadlines in mind*

  • Research colleges using the above criteria/website and make a tentative list of your choices
  • Visit colleges or go on a tour/college fair, talk to students/faculty, research and learn more before finalizing your list (Use the form below to help you stay organized!)
  • Look up requirements and deadlines for each schools such as letters of recommendation, standardized testing, and supplemental essays
  • Start on your common application  and supplemental applications
  • Send in all materials by the given deadlines
  • Check application portals and your email frequently
  • Do interviews
  • Send financial aid documents if needed such as CAS, TAP, FAFSA, IDOC (use form included below!)
  • Check your Student Aid Report when it comes for any discrepancies
  • Wait for results.... look at your options and decide on the college you will attend next year. CONGRATULATIONS!!



Choosing a school

Find your best fit through research! Use online websites such as this to find which college fits you. You will need to consider the following criteria.

College Type

  • Liberal arts colleges: small class size, focus on undergraduate studies
  • Universities: large class size, more resources
  • Technical institutes and professional schools: prepare you for specific career in specific field students have already chosen to pursue
  • Historically black colleges and universities: large black educational community
  • Tribal colleges: focus on American Indian students
  • Women's colleges: focus on serving female students
  • Community or junior schools: offer 2 years of liberal arts education
  • Proprietary institutions: under for-profit companies

Location

  • Urban/rural
  • Distance from home
  • Weather
  • Distance from cities, communities
  • Northeast/ Southeast/Midwest/Northwest/Overseas/Other

Atmosphere

  • Co-ed/male/female
  • Religious tradition
  • School spirit
  • Teaching style
  • Safety
  • Diversity

Selectivity and academics

  • Admission profile such as average test scores, GPA, and rank.
  • Distribution requirements
  • Offered majors
  • Class size
  • Special programs
  • Faculty-student relations

Expenses

  • Tuition fees
  • Room and board
  • Financial aid
  • Work-study
  • Available scholarships

Housing

  • Residence hall size
  • Food plan
  • Inside/outside campus

Extracurriculars

  • Internships
  • Clubs
  • Organizations
  • Greek life
  • Community service opportunities
  • Athletics
  • Intramural sports



Common application prompts

Start thinking about your essay and read some of these well-written essays. This will help you find out how to tackle the essays.

2017-18 Common App Essays

Nearly 700 colleges accept the The Common Application, which makes it easy to apply to multiple schools with just one form. If you are using the Common App to apply for college admission, you will have 250–650 words to respond to ONE of the following prompts:

  1. Some students have a background, identity, interest, or talent that is so meaningful they believe their application would be incomplete without it. If this sounds like you, then please share your story.
  2. The lessons we take from obstacles we encounter can be fundamental to later success. Recount a time when you faced a challenge, setback, or failure.  How did it affect you, and what did you learn from the experience?
  3. Reflect on a time when you questioned or challenged a belief or idea. What prompted your thinking? What was the outcome?
  4. Describe a problem you've solved or a problem you'd like to solve. It can be an intellectual challenge, a research query, an ethical dilemma—anything that is of personal importance, no matter the scale. Explain its significance to you and what steps you took or could be taken to identify a solution.
  5. Discuss an accomplishment, event, or realization that sparked a period of personal growth and a new understanding of yourself or others.
  6. Describe a topic, idea, or concept you find so engaging that it makes you lose all track of time. Why does it captivate you? What or who do you turn to when you want to learn more?
  7. Share an essay on any topic of your choice. It can be one you've already written, one that responds to a different prompt, or one of your own design.
  • Do not focus on just answering the question. Include personal achievements and show colleges why you are a good fit for them. Display positive qualities such as leadership, well-roundedness, passion, and teamwork.

Inerview Tips/Tricks

Review these questions and practice answering them in front of a mirror

  1. Tell me about yourself.
  2. What are you interesting in this college?
  3. What are your strengths and weaknesses?
  4. What can you contribute to this school?
  5. What would you change about your high school?
  6. What is your favorite book and why?
  7. What is a challenge you faced? How did you overcome it?
  8. Tell me about a time you failed.
  • Similar to essays, focus on exhibiting your positive qualities through personal experiences and anecdotes.
  • Wear formal attire and arrive early in order to leave a good impression.
